Precautionary Measures



Internal use
Essential oils should only be taken internally under the supervision of a doctor or experienced aromatherapist. Improper internal use can be dangerous.
Phototoxic oils
These are essential oils which increase the light-sensitivity of the skin, for example, the oils of citrus fruits, particularly bergamot, hypericum, verbena and angelica root. Such oils should not be used in direct sunlight.
Pregnancy
The following essential oils should be avoided during pregnancy: angelica, aniseed, basil, citronella, tarragon, eucalyptus, fennel, cumin, bay laurel, marjoram, clary sage, clove, rosemary, sage, thyme, juniper and hyssop. Chamomile and peppermint should
not be used in the first few months of pregnancy.
Children
Essential oils should be used sparingly on children. The dosage should always be halved. The use of essential oils is not recommended for babies. Essential oils must be kept out of reach of children.
Direct contact with the skin
Essential oils should always be diluted when used directly on the skin. Clove, verbena, cinnamon and lemongrass are particularly irritating to the skin. Those with sensitive skin or a tendency to allergies should make a test in the fold of the elbow before further use.
Epilepsy
Persons suffering from epilepsy should avoid the following essential oils: fennel, camphor, rosemary, sage, thyme, hyssop and cedar.
Homeopathy
Avoid using chamomile, camphor and peppermint essential oils during homeopathic treatment.
General
Essential oils should be stored away from direct sunlight, in a cool place and out of reach of children. Avoid contact with the eyes and mucus membrane. Essential oils are extremely inflammable and must be kept away from naked flames.
First Aid
Essential oils can best be applied to the skin when mixed with vegetable oils, butter or milk. In the event of skin irritation or burning, clean off the essential oil with a handkerchief soaked in vegetable oil and subsequently wash with plenty of water. In the event of eye contact, clean the eyes with plenty of vegetable oil (eg. Sunflower oil)
Emergencies
In case of extreme reactions (pain, cramps, dizziness, nausea) it is advisable to call a doctor.
